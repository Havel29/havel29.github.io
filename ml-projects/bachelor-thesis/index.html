Bachelor's Thesis: High-Performance Simulation of Compressible Flows

July 1, 2024
2 min read
Research Numerical Methods GPU Computing Fluid Dynamics

Overview

My bachelor’s thesis completed in 2024 at the Free University of Bolzano (graduated 110 cum laude). This research developed a high-performance numerical solver for simulating water flows in pipe networks, with applications to leak detection using machine learning.

  • Successfully simulated real-world networks with 1.5 million unknowns
  • Unconditionally stable semi-implicit discretization scheme
  • GPU acceleration with parallel computing techniques

Technical Highlights

The project tackles the challenging problem of simulating compressible fluid flows in systems of compliant elastic pipes. The solver uses:

  • Semi-implicit numerical methods for unconditional stability
  • Custom preconditioner based on the Thomas algorithm
  • GPU parallelization with CUDA
  • Finite-volume and upwind discretization schemes

The method demonstrated excellent convergence properties and numerical accuracy when validated against theoretical benchmarks and real-world networks like the Modena water distribution system.
